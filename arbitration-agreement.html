Arbitration Process

The arbitration process shall proceed as follows:

  1. Notice of Dispute: Either party may initiate arbitration by providing written notice to the other party
  2. Selection of Arbitrator: The parties shall select a mutually agreeable arbitrator within 30 days
  3. Arbitration Hearing: The hearing shall be conducted within 90 days of arbitrator selection
  4. Evidence and Testimony: Each party may present evidence and call witnesses
  5. Arbitrator Decision: The arbitrator shall render a written decision within 30 days of the hearing
  6. Binding Effect: The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on both parties

Limited Exceptions to Arbitration

The following disputes are NOT subject to arbitration and may be resolved in court:

  • Intellectual Property Disputes: Claims involving patents, trademarks, copyrights, or trade secrets
  • Injunctive Relief: Claims seeking injunctive or other equitable relief
  • Small Claims: Disputes involving less than $10,000 may be resolved in small claims court
  • Emergency Relief: Emergency or temporary restraining orders
  • Collection Actions: Actions to collect outstanding debts or fees

Arbitration Costs and Fees

Costs and fees associated with arbitration shall be allocated as follows:

  • Filing Fees: Each party shall pay their own filing fees
  • Arbitrator Fees: Shared equally between the parties
  • Attorney Fees: Each party shall bear their own attorney fees
  • Expert Witness Fees: Each party shall bear their own expert witness fees
  • Other Costs: Each party shall bear their own costs for discovery, transcripts, and other expenses

Confidentiality

All arbitration proceedings shall be confidential and private:

  • No public access to arbitration hearings or records
  • Arbitration decisions shall not be published or made public
  • All parties and witnesses are bound by confidentiality obligations
  • Arbitration records shall not be discoverable in other proceedings

If any provision of this Arbitration Agreement is found to be unenforceable or invalid:

  • The parties shall attempt to negotiate a replacement provision
  • If negotiation fails, the matter shall be resolved by the arbitrator

Modification and Termination

This Arbitration Agreement may be modified or terminated as follows:
