Force Majeure Clause

Protection from circumstances beyond reasonable control and unforeseeable events.

Last Updated: October 2025

This Force Majeure Clause ("Clause") is a legally binding provision that protects Michael Brennan ("Company," "we," "our," or "us") from liability for failure to perform obligations due to circumstances beyond our reasonable control.

Force Majeure means any event or circumstance that is beyond the reasonable control of the Company and that prevents or delays the performance of any obligation under any agreement, including but not limited to:

Specific Force Majeure Events

The following events are specifically included as Force Majeure events:

üö´ Covered Force Majeure Events

  • Natural Disasters: Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, wildfires, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions
  • Extreme Weather: Blizzards, ice storms, heat waves, droughts, and other extreme weather conditions
  • Acts of God: Lightning strikes, solar flares, meteor impacts, and other uncontrollable natural phenomena
  • War and Conflict: War, terrorism, civil unrest, riots, and military actions
  • Government Actions: Government shutdowns, regulatory changes, trade restrictions, and embargoes
  • Pandemics: Epidemics, pandemics, and public health emergencies
  • Infrastructure Failures: Power outages, internet outages, and telecommunications disruptions
  • Cyber Attacks: Large-scale cyber attacks, ransomware, and security breaches
  • Labor Disputes: Strikes, work stoppages, and labor disputes
  • Equipment Failures: Major equipment failures, server crashes, and technical malfunctions

Effect of Force Majeure

When a Force Majeure event occurs, the following provisions apply:

  • Performance Excused: The Company's performance of any affected obligation is excused
  • No Liability: The Company shall not be liable for any failure to perform due to Force Majeure
  • No Breach: Force Majeure events do not constitute a breach of any agreement
  • Extension of Time: Any time limits for performance are extended by the duration of the Force Majeure event
  • No Damages: No damages or penalties shall be assessed against the Company
  • No Termination: The other party may not terminate the agreement due to Force Majeure

Notice Requirements

When a Force Majeure event occurs, the Company shall:

  1. Immediate Notice: Provide written notice within 24 hours of becoming aware of the event
  2. Detailed Information: Include details about the event and its expected duration
  3. Regular Updates: Provide regular updates on the status of the event
  4. Resolution Notice: Notify when the event has been resolved
  5. Mitigation Efforts: Describe efforts being made to mitigate the effects

Mitigation Obligations

The Company agrees to use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of Force Majeure events:

  • Implementing backup systems and redundancy measures
  • Seeking alternative methods of performance
  • Working with third-party providers to restore services
  • Implementing emergency response procedures
  • Communicating with affected parties
  • Taking steps to prevent or minimize future occurrences

Duration and Termination

The following provisions apply to the duration of Force Majeure events:

  • Reasonable Duration: Force Majeure protection continues for a reasonable duration
  • Termination Right: Either party may terminate if the event continues for more than 90 days
  • Partial Performance: The Company may perform partially if possible
  • Resumption of Performance: Normal performance resumes when the event ends
  • No Penalties: No penalties or damages for delayed performance

Exclusions from Force Majeure

The following events are NOT considered Force Majeure events:

  • Economic downturns or market fluctuations
  • Changes in business conditions or demand
  • Financial difficulties or insolvency
  • Labor disputes involving the Company's own employees
  • Equipment failures due to lack of maintenance
  • Software bugs or programming errors
  • Third-party service failures due to contract disputes
  • Events that could have been prevented with reasonable care

Consequential Damages

In the event of Force Majeure, the Company shall not be liable for:

  • Lost profits or revenue
  • Lost business opportunities
  • Consequential or indirect damages
  • Punitive or exemplary damages
  • Emotional distress or mental anguish
  • Loss of data or digital content
  • Third-party claims or damages
  • Any other damages not directly caused by the Company

Insurance and Indemnification

The following provisions apply to insurance and indemnification:

  • Insurance Coverage: The Company maintains appropriate insurance coverage
  • Indemnification: Users agree to indemnify the Company for Force Majeure-related claims
  • Third-Party Claims: The Company is not responsible for third-party claims arising from Force Majeure
  • Subrogation: Insurance companies may not subrogate against the Company

Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Force Majeure Clause shall be governed by:

  • Governing Law: The laws of the State of Delaware
  • Dispute Resolution: Any disputes shall be resolved through arbitration
  • Jurisdiction: The state and federal courts of Delaware
  • Severability: If any provision is invalid, the remainder remains in effect

Modification and Waiver

This Force Majeure Clause may be modified or waived as follows:

  • Modification: Only by written agreement signed by both parties
  • Waiver: No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing
  • Partial Waiver: Waiver of one provision does not waive others
  • No Course of Dealing: Past performance does not waive future rights
